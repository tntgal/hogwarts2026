A Letter from Your Host

The magic begins again — this time, we go back to where it all started.

Dear friends and fellow witches and wizards,


Every year on July 31st — the birthday of the Boy Who Lived — we gather to celebrate the magic of Harry Potter. This year, we return to the very beginning: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone. Whether this is your first time reading it or your tenth, there is always something new to discover in those enchanted pages.


This guide is your companion from now until the party. Included you will find two reading tracks, three summer challenges, a Sorting Quiz to discover your Hogwarts house, Movie Night details, and everything you need to earn House Points before the celebration.


Every person will be sorted into their own Hogwarts house — no matter your age, no matter who you came with. The Points Passport was tucked inside the printed packet. Make sure to fill it out all summer long. Bring it to the party and watch your house's jewel vase grow.


Your journey begins now.

Throughout the summer, owls will deliver reminders about the reading schedule and upcoming challenges — so nothing sneaks up on you. And a very special owl will deliver some very important mail the night before the party for those who are attending. Keep an eye on your door.

The Jewel Hourglasses

At the party, four glass vases — one per house — filled with colored gems serve as the official House Point display. Gryffindor red, Slytherin green, Ravenclaw blue, Hufflepuff yellow. Each gem represents 5 points. Every point earned over the summer is loaded in before the first guest arrives, and gems continue to be added throughout the night as points are awarded. The fuller the vase, the stronger the magic.

Owl Post — A Letter to Tracy

Write a letter or create a drawing inspired by something from the book — a scene, a character, a magical object, a moment that stayed with you. Fold it up, address it to Tracy, and sneak it under her doormat before the party.

  • Write it in character as a Hogwarts student, or simply as yourself
  • Any medium welcome — pencil sketch, watercolor, notebook paper, parchment
  • No photo needed. No posting required. Just the quiet magic of a letter delivered by hand.
A note

This one is for everyone — the quiet ones, the shy ones, the ones who prefer to create privately. There is no wrong way to do it and no audience required.

Character Commitment

Create your own magical character from the wizarding world. One caveat - kids are kids (students) and adults are adults (professors, muggles, Ministry of Magic professionals). Draw or write a description with as much or little detail as you'd like. It is strongly encouraged that your dress up as your character at the party — but the only requirement is that you show up with magic in your heart.

  • Consider your companion — a toad, cat, owl, rat, turtle, dog, duck, or any creature you would bring to Hogwarts!
  • Choose a name or modify your own.
  • What's your style? Talents? Background?

Milestone Challenges

Three challenges over the summer. Complete them for bonus House Points.

How challenges work

Each challenge is sent on its dispatch date. You have one week to complete it. Submissions can go to the group chat, or privately to Tracy by text or photo — both earn full points. No pressure, no performance. Just magic.

The optional group chat

A GroupMe link will be shared with all guests. Joining is completely optional — it is a space to share challenge submissions, cheer each other on, and follow the house standings all summer. If you would rather keep things private, submit directly to Tracy instead. Both paths earn full points and are equally welcome.

Sent June 7 · Due June 14 · 15 House Points

Challenge 1 — The Spell-Casting Dare

You have just read about Harry's first weeks at Hogwarts, where magic becomes real. Now it is your turn.

  • Choose a spell from Chapters 1–8, or invent your own original incantation entirely
  • Perform it on camera — or describe it in writing if video feels uncomfortable
  • Name the spell, explain what it does, and deliver the incantation with full commitment
  • Share to the group chat, or send directly to Tracy by June 14
Not a video person?

Write your spell on paper — name, incantation, wand movement, and effect — and slip it under Tracy's doormat. Counts for full points.

Sent June 28 · Due July 5 · 15 House Points

Challenge 2 — The Hogwarts Scavenger Hunt

Somewhere in or around your home, the following magical items are hiding. Find something that represents each one.

  • Something that could be an Invisibility Cloak — any translucent or sheer fabric
  • Something that could be a broomstick
  • Something that could be a golden snitch — small, round, gold-ish
  • Something with wings or that could fly
  • A mirror — in honor of the Mirror of Erised

Lay them all out and photograph the collection, or photograph each one separately. Share to the group chat or send to Tracy privately by July 5.

Sent July 12 · Due July 17 · 20 House Points

Challenge 3 — The Final Riddle

Five questions drawn from the book. No searching online — your memory and your reading are your only tools. Several answers differ between the book and the film.

  • What are the six enchanted obstacles, in order, leading to the Stone?
  • What does the Mirror of Erised show Harry when he stands before it at the very end?
  • What is the inscription above the Mirror of Erised, and what does it reveal when reversed?
  • Who gave Harry his Invisibility Cloak, and whose was it originally?
  • What are the first words Harry says to the Sorting Hat before it places him?

Send these answers directly to Tracy by July 17 so answers aren't spoiled for those in the group chat. Only those who read the book will know these.
